Case Studies

In-depth analysis of existing conservation DAOs and their applications

This appendix provides detailed case studies of four existing conservation DAOs, analyzing their structures, operations, impacts, and lessons for Re:Wild's potential DAO implementations.

Case Study 1: Carbonland DAO

Overview

Mission: Carbonland DAO aims to protect forests and their carbon sequestration capacity through collective ownership and management of forest land.

Founded: 2022

Blockchain Platform: Polygon

Website: https://carbonlanddao.com/

Key Features:
  • Tokenized land ownership through NFTs
  • Carbon credit generation and distribution
  • Decentralized land management decisions
  • Transparent impact reporting
Token Structure:
  • LAND NFTs: Represent ownership shares of specific forest parcels
  • CARBON tokens: Fungible tokens representing verified carbon credits
  • CARB governance tokens: Voting rights for DAO decisions
Organizational Structure

Carbonland DAO operates through a hybrid structure:

Legal Structure:
  • Wyoming DAO LLC for legal entity status
  • Traditional land trusts for property ownership
  • Non-profit foundation for grant management
Governance Structure:
  • Token-weighted voting for major decisions
  • Expert council for technical oversight
  • Working groups for specific functions
  • Local stewardship committees
Decision-Making Process:
  1. Proposal submission (requires minimum token stake)
  2. Community discussion period (minimum 7 days)
  3. Expert review and recommendation
  4. Token-weighted voting (minimum 14 days)
  5. Implementation by working groups
Operations and Impact
Current Operations:
  • 3,500 acres of forest land under protection
  • $2.4 million raised through token sales
  • 4,200 community members
  • 12 conservation projects funded
Impact Metrics:
  • 42,000 tons of carbon sequestered annually
  • 3 endangered species habitats protected
  • 27 local jobs created
  • $180,000 in carbon credit revenue generated
Technical Implementation:
  • Smart contracts for token issuance and governance
  • Integration with carbon credit verification platforms
  • Satellite monitoring for forest health
  • Transparent dashboard for impact tracking
Challenges and Lessons
Key Challenges:
  • Regulatory complexity of land ownership
  • Balancing token value with conservation goals
  • Technical barriers for community participation
  • Ensuring long-term financial sustainability
Lessons for Re:Wild:
  • Hybrid legal structures are essential
  • Local community integration is critical
  • Technical simplicity improves participation
  • Multiple revenue streams ensure sustainability

Case Study 2: KlimaDAO

Overview

Mission: KlimaDAO aims to accelerate the price appreciation of carbon assets by creating a liquid carbon-backed currency and ecosystem, driving climate action.

Founded: 2021

Blockchain Platform: Polygon

Website: https://www.klimadao.finance/

Key Features:
  • Carbon-backed treasury and currency
  • Automated market operations
  • Staking and bonding mechanisms
  • Carbon retirement functionality
Token Structure:
  • KLIMA: Carbon-backed currency token
  • sKLIMA: Staked KLIMA that earns rebase rewards
  • BCT: Base Carbon Tonne (tokenized carbon credits)
Relevance to Conservation

While KlimaDAO is primarily focused on carbon markets rather than direct conservation, it offers several important lessons for conservation DAOs:

Market Mechanisms:
  • Creating liquidity for environmental assets
  • Price discovery for ecosystem services
  • Automated market operations
  • Treasury management strategies
Conservation Applications:
  • Funding mechanism for carbon-sequestering projects
  • Model for other ecosystem service tokens
  • Integration with existing carbon markets
  • Transparent impact accounting
Lessons for Re:Wild
  • Market integration: Connecting conservation outcomes to existing markets can drive significant capital
  • Tokenomics design: Careful economic design is critical for sustainable operations
  • Transparency: On-chain transparency builds trust with stakeholders
  • Regulatory navigation: Proactive engagement with regulators is essential
  • Community building: Strong community engagement drives participation and advocacy

Case Study 3: MesoReefDAO

Overview

Mission: MesoReefDAO focuses on regenerating coral reef conservation through decentralized science (DeSci) and Web3 tools.

Founded: 2023

Blockchain Platform: Ethereum/Polygon

Website: https://mesoreefdao.org/

Key Features:
  • Regenerative science framework
  • Decentralized research funding
  • Community-driven conservation projects
  • Integration of DeSci and ReFi principles
Focus Areas:
  • Coral reef restoration
  • Marine biodiversity monitoring
  • Sustainable coastal community development
  • Ocean conservation research
Innovative Approaches
Decentralized Science:
  • Open-source research protocols
  • Community peer review
  • Tokenized research contributions
  • Data ownership through NFTs
Regenerative Finance:
  • Sustainable funding mechanisms
  • Value alignment with ecological health
  • Community-owned conservation assets
  • Transparent impact measurement
Lessons for Re:Wild
  • Scientific integration: Combining decentralized governance with scientific rigor
  • Community science: Engaging non-experts in meaningful data collection and analysis
  • Ecosystem approach: Addressing conservation challenges holistically
  • Focused geography: Starting with specific ecosystems before expanding
  • Educational component: Building awareness alongside direct conservation action

Case Study 4: Big Green DAO

Overview

Mission: Big Green DAO is the first non-profit led philanthropic DAO, focused on food justice and sustainable gardening initiatives across the United States.

Founded: 2021

Blockchain Platform: Ethereum

Website: https://dao.biggreen.org/

Key Features:
  • Decentralized grantmaking for food projects
  • Community governance of funding decisions
  • Transparent impact reporting
  • Non-profit integration with DAO structure
Target Audience:
  • Food justice organizations
  • Community garden initiatives
  • Sustainable agriculture projects
  • Environmental education programs
Governance Model

Big Green DAO has developed an innovative governance model that combines traditional non-profit oversight with decentralized decision-making:

Governance Structure:
  • Non-profit foundation oversight
  • Community-led proposal review
  • Transparent voting mechanisms
  • Working groups for specific focus areas
Decision Process:
  • Open proposal submissions
  • Community discussion and refinement
  • Voting by DAO members
  • Implementation and monitoring
Lessons for Re:Wild
  • Non-profit integration: Successfully combining traditional non-profit structures with DAO governance
  • Community empowerment: Shifting decision-making power to those most affected by the issues
  • Transparent grantmaking: Creating open processes for fund allocation
  • Regulatory compliance: Navigating legal requirements while maintaining decentralization
  • Impact measurement: Developing effective metrics for environmental and social outcomes

Key Takeaways for Re:Wild

  1. Hybrid structures are essential: All successful conservation DAOs maintain some traditional legal and organizational elements alongside decentralized components.
  2. Multiple revenue streams ensure sustainability: Reliance on a single funding mechanism creates vulnerability; diversified approaches are more resilient.
  3. Technical simplicity improves participation: User experience must be accessible to non-technical conservation stakeholders.
  4. Local community integration is critical: Conservation DAOs must meaningfully involve local communities in both governance and benefits.
  5. Transparent impact verification builds trust: Clear, verifiable reporting on conservation outcomes is essential for maintaining supporter engagement.
  6. Governance design must balance expertise with decentralization: Effective conservation DAOs incorporate scientific and local expertise while maintaining community governance.
  7. Regulatory navigation requires proactive engagement: Working with regulators early in the process prevents later complications.
